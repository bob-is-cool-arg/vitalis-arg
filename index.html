Author: Bob Williams

Date: 04/29

The ongoing experiments have reached a critical phase. The progress is undeniable, but so are the risks. Last week’s incident still weighs heavily on me. Security protocols failed, and there was a breach. It's becoming harder to justify the cost of human life against what we’re trying to achieve.

On a personal note, I’m reminded daily of my responsibilities as a father. Ava would be devastated if she understood the extent of my involvement in this project. Her name and birth year have become an ironic choice for security credentials: *Ava-1994.* It’s both a comfort and a reminder of what’s at stake.

I’ve kept her in the dark, but for how long? The weight of these secrets is unbearable. It’s clear that what we’ve created here is something far beyond our comprehension. The reports are troubling—subjects exhibiting unusual behaviors, the anomalies spreading outside our controlled environment.

I’ve been reviewing old entries, trying to make sense of it all. How did we get here? What began as a quest for knowledge has turned into something much darker. If anyone finds this, please remember one thing: I did this for her. For Ava.
